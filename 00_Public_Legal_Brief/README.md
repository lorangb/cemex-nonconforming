# Public Legal Brief — The Case for Terminating CEMEX Lyons' Nonconforming Use

A plain-language, citation-backed synthesis of the record and the strongest argument that the
CEMEX Lyons cement plant and quarry forfeited their protected "nonconforming use" status by
unlawfully expanding and intensifying over decades.

## The argument in one line
A nonconforming use may **continue** but may not be **enlarged or intensified**. CEMEX's truck
traffic over Highway 66 roughly **doubled** after the Dowe Flats quarry closed in 2022 — far
beyond the **1994 baseline**, when raw materials were supplied locally, not by truck. Boulder
County rightly terminated the nonconforming use in **April 2024**; the **May 2026 rescission**
rests on a CEMEX traffic study (Landis) that **omits the 1994 data**. This brief makes the case
to **reverse the rescission** — with physical expansion and the 1997–2000 Clean Air Act
violations as independent, supporting grounds.

## Key authorities
- Boulder County Land Use Code, Article 4 (nonconforming structures & uses; § 4-604 lapse).
- *Save Our Saint Vrain Valley, Inc. v. Boulder Cnty. Board of Adjustment*, 19CA2251 (Colo. App. 2021).
- *Sierra Club v. Billingsley*, 166 P.3d 309 (Colo. App. 2007) — distinguished.
- *United States v. CEMEX, Inc.* (Lyons) — 2013 Clean Air Act consent decree; U.S. DOJ/EPA.
